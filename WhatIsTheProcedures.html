What is the procedure?

What is the procedure?

Upon contacting Eunoia Clinic, our receptionist will set up an appointment for you with a therapist to whom you have been referred or one that best matches your areas of concerns for treatment.

At your first appointment, your therapist will discuss confidentiality of treatment information. Your therapist will also fully discuss your areas of concerns, the services they offer, and begin to set up a detailed plan to meet your treatment goals. A referral to psychiatry for a medication evaluation may also be made at this time, if necessary.

Confidentiality

In the usual course of events, you have the right to keep your therapy content here completely private.
This means that, without your written permission, no information about your contact with Eunoia
is available to anyone outside of the clinic, including parents
(if you are over 18), family members, friends, or outside agencies.

Exceptions to Confidentiality

With the exception of specific legal circumstances described below, you have the right to the confidentiality
of your therapy and therapy records unless you provide written consent for disclosure.
The following are legal exceptions to your right to confidentiality that we will discuss should the need arise:

If you pose a threat of harm to yourself, to another person,
we will take whatever steps are required or permitted by law,
to help prevent the potential harm from happening.

This may include contacting your family and/or other medical professionals;

If you report information indicating that a child, disabled, or elderly person is suffering abuse or neglect;

A court order, issued by a judge, could require us to release information contained in your records, or could require a therapist to testify.

INFORMED CONSENT:

Upon registration, you will be asked to sign an informed consent. It is an agreement between client and clinician
that will grant authorization to provide evaluation and treatment services for your care.
It indicates you have read and understand policy surrounding confidentiality and its limits and clarifies your
commitment to participate in treatment planning to the best of your ability.
Finally, informed consent will indicate that you understand that therapy may bring up
difficult thoughts or feelings and to recognize that receiving therapy is not a guaranteed cure.

What is expected from a client?

Therapy may not be effective for everyone,
but you can take steps to get the most out of your therapy and help make it a success.

Approach therapy as a partnership.
Therapy is most effective when you're an active participant and share in decision-making.

Be open and honest. Success with therapy depends on your willingness to share your thoughts,
feelings and experiences, and on being open to new insights and ways of doing things.
Let your therapist know about your reservations especially if itâ€™s related to difficulties in expressing emotions
that are challenging to you. Try to attend all sessions and give some thought to what you want to discuss.

Don't expect instant results. Working on emotional issues can be painful and often requires hard work.
It's not uncommon to feel worse during the initial part of therapy as you begin to confront past and current
conflicts. You may need several sessions before you begin to see improvement.

If you don't feel that you're benefiting from therapy after several sessions, talk to your therapist about it.
You and your therapist may decide to make some changes or try a different approach.
